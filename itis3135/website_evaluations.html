Website Evaluations

Van Gogh Exhibit Charlotte

This nice looking site is for a Van Gogh immersive exhibit here in Charlotte, NC. It is a simple website with the purpse of selling tickets for the exhibit as well as advertise and have info for events and activities to do at the exhibit, and a good example of a page following the CRAP principles.

  1. Contrast: This page has consistent and easy to read contrast for it's visuals and text. It's colors for each section alter between light and dark backgrounds, which help to break up sections of the site at the expense of being slightly blinding.
  2. Repetition: The layout and lists of information on this page are repeated in different forms of cards, which help the reader understand and know what to expect.
  3. Alignment: This site makes great use of negative space, through the use of images relating to the content being advertised. Every element is aligned and connected with similar elements, and everything feels like it makes sense with where it is on the page.
  4. Proximity: This page is pretty responsive and readable on a phone and on a big screen, thanks to its centered approach with seperates bodies vertically, displaying as many elements horizontally in each body as readable for the screen width. There is an acceptable amount of space between elements that enhance it visually.

This site is the main page page for the Yale School of Art, and has an index with places for students and faculty. It is an example of a poorly designed website in reference to the CRAP principles. It is designed by students and every student has access to be able to edit the style for the site.

  1. Contrast: The color on this site is not cohesive, with stark differences in levels of contrast that go from blinding like in the headings, to messy with how the lower headings for the newsletters that blend in with the background.
  2. Repetition: This website does have repetition, just not in the pleasing to the eyes way. The background gradients repeat for multiple sections, as does the gif used for the background.
  3. Alignment: The page is skewed to the left of center, with a shortr side bar with links and two columns to right that go to the bottom of the page. The heights of the bodys in the columns are not aligned and many are mismatched.
  4. Proximity: This site page is filled with text that is hard to read, and many links to information and related sites in different places. This is expected as the site has many purposes and editors.
